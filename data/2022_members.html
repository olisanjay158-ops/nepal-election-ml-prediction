The Second Federal Parliament of Nepal, was elected by the 2022 general elections on 20 November 2022.[1] The elections elected 275 Members of Parliament (MPs), 165 for each constituency and 110 through the party list, to the House of Representatives.[2] The parliament convened for the first time on 9 January 2023 and was dissolved on 12 September 2025 following the 2025 Gen Z protests.[3][4]
